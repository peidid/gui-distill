GUI AGENT DISTILLATION — RESULTS SUMMARY (learning run: Tracks A & B)
Updated 2026-06-02
================================================================================

GOAL
  Black-box hard-label distillation of a mobile GUI agent: train a small student
  to imitate a teacher's TEXT actions (no logits), then measure WHERE the small
  model falls short — grounding (perception) vs multi-step (control).

SETUP (identical across tracks unless noted)
  Student : Qwen2.5-VL-3B-Instruct, LoRA SFT (rank 16, alpha 32, lr 1e-4,
            2 epochs, bf16, vision tower frozen; ~30M trainable = 0.79%).
            Trained on a rented RTX 4090.
  Design  : vary ONLY the teacher (the source of action labels); same student,
            recipe, training states, and eval.
              Track A teacher = AndroidControl human demonstrations (~error-free).
              Track B teacher = UI-TARS-7B-DPO (open GUI agent); teacher-forced
                                relabel of the SAME states (run_teacher.py;
                                92.9% parse coverage, 4798/5167 steps kept).
  Data    : AndroidControl (Google public bucket). Train = 800 episodes /
            5167 steps. Test = 200 DISJOINT episodes / 1332 steps
            (--skip_episodes 800; verified train/test episode overlap = 0).
  Eval    : ScreenSpot-V2 grounding (400 samples; click must land in GT box) and
            AndroidControl-test step accuracy (teacher-forced; action TYPE must
            match AND args: click in box/radius, text/dir/app string match).
  COORDS  : Qwen2.5-VL emits ABSOLUTE PIXELS (not 0-1000). Both students KEPT the
            pixel convention -> scored --coord_space pixel. UI-TARS emits 0-1000
            -> scored norm. (Track-B student: pixel 0.280 vs norm 0.065 confirms
            pixel.) Verified from raw outputs; mis-scoring the space reads ~0.

FINDINGS
================================================================================
1) TEACHER-ERROR / COVERAGE INHERITANCE (headline).
   The Track-B student fails exactly the actions its teacher cannot emit:
       done    : A 0.815 -> B 0.000   (teacher 0.005)
       open_app: A 0.827 -> B 0.133   (teacher 0.000)
   UI-TARS has no open_app action and rarely signals completion, so the student
   distilled from it never learns to open apps or terminate — while the
   human-taught student masters both. Black-box hard-label distillation
   transmits the teacher's blind spots directly.

2) MULTI-STEP TRACKS THE TEACHER.
   student B overall (0.319) ~= teacher (0.316); student A (0.552) tracks its
   (human) teacher. The student's multi-step competence is bounded by its
   teacher -> a better teacher yields a better student on control.

3) GROUNDING DOES NOT TRANSFER.
   Both students land at EXACTLY 0.280 on ScreenSpot regardless of teacher, and
   the teacher's far-better grounding (0.650) did NOT carry over. Grounding
   DEGRADED from base (0.603 -> 0.280) — a property of the fine-tuning
   recipe/distribution (narrow phone-GUI SFT, frozen vision, a light LoRA that
   keeps the base's coordinate convention), NOT of the label source.
   Nuance: in-distribution click IMPROVED over base (0.125 -> 0.478 A / 0.391 B);
   the ScreenSpot drop is OUT-OF-DISTRIBUTION (desktop/web) grounding.

INTERPRETATION
   Distillation here transmits PROCEDURE (which action, in-distribution) far more
   than PERCEPTION (general grounding), and faithfully inherits the teacher's
   action-space coverage gaps. The "cost" of black-box hard-label distillation
   concentrates in (a) out-of-distribution grounding and (b) capabilities the
   teacher cannot demonstrate.

CAVEATS
   - Offline teacher-forced step accuracy measures AGREEMENT with the single
     human reference action; it favors the human-distilled student and penalizes
     a capable-but-different agent. UI-TARS's low AndroidControl number (0.316)
     is largely action-space mismatch (no open_app, different termination), NOT
     incompetence — its grounding (0.650) leads all models.
   - Single run, no seeds/error bars (pilot). ScreenSpot is OOD vs the phone
     training data. Teachers are OPEN (learning run); the paper's premise needs a
     genuinely CLOSED API teacher.
